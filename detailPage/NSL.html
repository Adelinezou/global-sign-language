Nepali Sign Language (NSL)

Nepali Sign Language (NSL) is the sign language used by the Deaf community in Nepal. It is a fully developed language with its own grammar and vocabulary, distinct from spoken Nepali.

History and Origin

NSL has evolved from a combination of local sign languages and gestures used by the Deaf community in Nepal. Its development has been influenced by the cultural and linguistic diversity of the country.

Usage and Community

NSL is used by Deaf individuals and their families, as well as interpreters and educators. The language serves as a crucial means of communication and is integral to the identity of the Deaf community in Nepal.

Distinct Features of NSL

  • NSL is a visual language that relies on hand signs, facial expressions, and body movements.
  • The grammar of NSL differs from spoken Nepali, utilizing its own syntax and structure.
  • Facial expressions are vital for conveying tone and context.
  • Fingerspelling is used for spelling out names and specific terms not represented by established signs.

NSL in Education

NSL is increasingly being recognized in educational settings for Deaf students in Nepal, allowing for improved access to education in their native language.
